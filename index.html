One thought leader and entrepreneur who inspired me is Mike Bostock.

The name will probably not ring a bell unless you work in data visualization, but he is well-known in that domain.

He created the influential open-source data visualization library for the web known as D3, or data driven documents.

He also created Observable, a data visualization platform for sharing data insights collaboratively via notebooks.

More than a decade ago in 2013, a younger me was inspired by his brilliant work he did at the New York Times then, and his work sent me down a rabbit hole wondering how the hell I could do something just as beautiful.

At that time I was working in public relations at PR agency GolinHarris, but I was bored and unfufilled. As an ex-journalist and storyteller, the data visualizations spoke to me.


So I quit and started my own small data visualization studio VslashR, which I would not suggest to anyone who needs to pay bills.

I really had little idea of what I was doing then, but somehow survived as a small business, which I attribute to more luck than grit.

Looking back it has been a really fufilling decade-plus of learning how to code again, meeting great clients and friends, running data visualization meetup events (still ongoing), and in the last five years I've taught data viz at universities as well.

As an engineer, I have deep respect for thought leaders who put in the work and time to build great products. People who are not just ego-driven attention seekers that only worship at the altar of money.

Better still if the work is shared freely and open-source, multiplying the impact and benefit. So thank you Mike Bostock.


In tech, like in many other domains, we stand on the shoulders of giants.

Without Linus Torvalds we wouldn't have Linux or Git. No Bitcoin or Blockchain without Satoshi. Even in the capitalist world there would be no Apple without Steve Wozniak.

As I grow older I appreciate community and the interconnectedness of our world more.

As the oft-quoted aphorism goes: "If you want to go fast, go alone. If you want to go far, go together."

I hope to find interesting people who might think like this in my one year Masters journey here. Thank you.
